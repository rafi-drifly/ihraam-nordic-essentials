The Ihram itself, the two unstitched white pieces of cloth, is identical for Umrah and Hajj. The cloth, the wrapping, and the rules of behaviour are the same. What changes is how long you stay in Ihram, the number of rites you complete in it, and consequently how many sets you should bring.

This is a practical comparison for pilgrims who may be making one journey now and the other in future, or who are deciding whether to buy a set that will serve both.

Is Ihram for Umrah the same as Ihram for Hajj?

Yes. The physical Ihram is the same. Both are two unstitched pieces of white cloth (the Izaar around the waist and the Ridaa over the shoulders), worn the same way, with the same prohibitions on scent, stitched clothing, hair removal, and other actions while in the Ihram state.

What differs is the duration and intensity of wear. Umrah typically lasts a few hours; Hajj keeps a pilgrim in Ihram across multiple days at Mina, Arafah, and Muzdalifah.

Time spent in Ihram: Umrah vs Hajj

The practical difference shows in the duration.

Umrah

A pilgrim enters Ihram at Miqat, performs Tawaf and Sa'i, then exits Ihram by shaving or trimming hair. Total time in Ihram is typically 3 to 6 hours, sometimes longer if there are crowds. One Ihram set is usually enough.

Hajj

A pilgrim enters Ihram at Miqat (or before, depending on the type of Hajj: Tamattu, Ifrad, or Qiran), then stays in Ihram across the days of Mina, Arafah, and Muzdalifah, exiting after Ramy al-Jamarat and the first phase of shaving. Total time in Ihram is several days. A spare set is strongly recommended.
