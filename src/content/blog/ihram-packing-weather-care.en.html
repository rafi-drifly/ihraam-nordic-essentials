The rulings of ihram get most of the attention, but the questions that keep first-time pilgrims up at night are practical ones. Will I roast in the heat? Should I buy my ihram at home or in Makkah? How many sets do I bring, and can I just machine wash it?

This guide answers the logistics so you can pack once and travel with confidence.

What's the weather like in Makkah, will I be too hot or cold?

For most of the year, the honest answer is: hot, and often very hot. Makkah sits in a dry desert valley, and heat is the defining challenge of the pilgrimage for the majority of visitors.

  • Summer (roughly June to September): Peak heat. Daytime highs commonly reach 40 to 43°C (104 to 109°F), and direct sun on marble makes it feel hotter. The most intense window is about 10am to 8pm.
  • Winter (roughly December to February): The most comfortable period, with daytime averages around the high 20s to low 30s °C, cooling at night.
  • Spring and autumn: Transitional and warm, between the two extremes.

Heat is not a minor comfort issue. Recent Hajj seasons have seen serious, even fatal, heat-related illness during the hottest months, so hydration and sun protection are essential. Drink water constantly, use shaded walkways, rest during peak hours where the rites allow, and do not push through dizziness. If you travel with elderly relatives, young children, or anyone with heart, kidney, or respiratory conditions, take extra care in summer. This is also why breathable, quick-drying ihram matters more than any other feature for most pilgrims.

Should I buy ihram before travel or in Saudi Arabia?

You can do either, but buying before you travel is usually the calmer choice.

Buying before travel

  • You choose the fabric, weave, and size unhurried, and can wash and prepare it at home.
  • You can practise wrapping the izar and rida before you are jet-lagged at the miqat.
  • You avoid the common scramble of shopping in an unfamiliar city right before entering ihram.
  • For pilgrims flying from Europe, ordering online with time to spare removes a real stress point. Pure Ihram, for example, ships fast within the EU.

Buying in Saudi Arabia

  • Ihram is widely available in Makkah and Jeddah, so you are never truly stuck.
  • Prices and quality vary, and you will be shopping under time pressure and in the heat.
  • You may not get to try sizes or choose the fabric you would have preferred.

How do I pack it, and how many sets should I bring?

How many sets:

  • Umrah: Bring two sets per man, one working set, one spare.
  • Hajj: Bring two to three sets per man for the hot days across Mina, Arafat, and Muzdalifah.
  • Women: Pack two or more modest outfits plus extra underclothes.

How to pack it:

  • Fold ihram flat and keep at least one set in your carry-on, in case checked luggage is delayed.
  • Add an ihram belt (with pockets for documents and cash) and comfortable open sandals for men.
  • Include unscented soap, unscented detergent or travel wash sachets, and a lightweight laundry bag.

Care instructions, can I machine wash it?

Yes, ihram cloth can normally be machine washed. It is ordinary fabric with no special sanctity in the material, so standard laundering is fine. A few sensible habits protect the weave and keep it fresh:

  • Wash on a gentle cycle with a mild detergent.
  • Wash white ihram separately or with other whites to keep it bright.
  • Dry it fully before packing so it never develops a musty smell.
  • Avoid harsh bleach on soft weaves like terry or microfiber over time.

Because exact care limits vary between fabrics and brands, follow the care guidance on the product you actually buy rather than a one-size-fits-all temperature.

What are the rules about scented clothing or detergents?

This is the care point with a religious dimension, and it catches many pilgrims out. Once you are in the state of ihram, perfume and scent are prohibited, including on your clothing. So the key timing rule is:

  • Before entering ihram: You may apply perfume to your body (a recommended sunnah for men), and normally scented clothes are no problem.
  • When you wash the ihram you will wear: Use unscented detergent so the cloth does not carry a fragrance into the sacred state. Avoid strongly perfumed powder or scented fabric softener.
  • During ihram: Do not apply perfume, scented lotion, scented soap, or scented deodorant. Pack unscented toiletries.
