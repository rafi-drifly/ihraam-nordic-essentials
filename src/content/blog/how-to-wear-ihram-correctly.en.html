Wearing Ihram correctly is simpler than it looks, but the first time can feel uncertain. The two unstitched pieces, the Izaar around the waist and the Ridaa over the shoulders, wrap rather than fasten, and the rules around what you can and cannot do once you're wearing them are part of the rite itself.

This is a step-by-step guide to wearing Ihram correctly for Hajj or Umrah, written for first-time pilgrims travelling from Europe. It covers the practical wrapping technique, the rules of the Ihram state, and the small things that go wrong if you skip the preparation.

What is Ihram?

Ihram is both a state and a set of clothing. The state of Ihram is the spiritual condition a pilgrim enters before crossing the Miqat boundary on the way to Makkah, marked by the niyyah (intention) and the recitation of the Talbiyah. The clothing is the two unstitched white pieces of cloth worn during this state by male pilgrims.

Female pilgrims do not wear the two-piece Ihram. They enter the state of Ihram in their normal modest clothing, with the rules of Ihram applied to behaviour rather than dress.

How to wear Ihram step by step

Before you start: perform ghusl (a full ritual bath), and wear the Ihram set you have already washed at home. The full step-by-step:

Step 1: Prepare the cloth

Lay both pieces, the Izaar (waist piece) and the Ridaa (shoulder piece), flat. Each Pure Ihram piece is 115 x 230 cm of soft microfiber. The cloth should already have been washed once at home to remove manufacturing residue.

Step 2: Wrap the Izaar

Hold the Izaar horizontally behind your back. Bring both ends to the front. Tuck the right side over the left at the waist, then tuck the left over the right, securing it with a belt. The cloth should sit between your navel and ankles, never above the navel, never dragging on the ground.

Step 3: Drape the Ridaa

Place the Ridaa over both shoulders so the cloth hangs evenly down your back and chest. During Tawaf for men, the right shoulder is uncovered (called Idtiba) by sliding the cloth under the right armpit and resting it on the left shoulder. After Tawaf, return the Ridaa to covering both shoulders.

Step 4: Sandals and final check

Wear sandals or flip-flops with no covered toes. Check that your Izaar is secure at the waist and that the Ridaa is not slipping. You are now ready to make the niyyah and recite the Talbiyah.

What you can and cannot do in Ihram

Once in the state of Ihram, the following are forbidden until the rites are complete and you exit the state.

  • Do not cut, pluck, or shave any hair from the body or head.
  • Do not trim or cut nails.
  • Do not wear scented products, including soap, deodorant, perfume, or scented sunscreen.
  • Do not cover the head (for men) with anything that touches the head directly.
  • Do not wear stitched clothing (for men), including shirts, trousers, and underwear.
  • Do not engage in marital relations or marriage contracts.
  • Do not hunt or harm wildlife within the Haram boundaries.
  • Do not engage in arguments, swearing, or disputes.

Common mistakes to avoid when wearing Ihram

These are the small things first-time pilgrims learn the hard way at Miqat. Knowing them ahead of time saves time and embarrassment.

Wearing it for the first time at Miqat

Practice wrapping at home at least once. The first time at Miqat, with limited space and a queue, is not the moment to learn.

Not pre-washing the cloth

Manufacturing residue on new Ihram causes itching and chafing during the long Tawaf circuits. One home wash with mild detergent fixes this. Pure Ihram does not pre-wash before shipping, so you do it yourself.

Not bringing a spare

A torn or stained Izaar at Mina with no spare in your suitcase is a stressful problem to solve. The 2-pack at EUR 37 is built for exactly this scenario.

Wearing scented products earlier in the day

The prohibition on scent applies once you enter Ihram, but lingering deodorant or perfume can technically violate the rule. Wash thoroughly before changing into Ihram and use only unscented soap.

Frequently asked questions

What is the correct way to wear Ihram for men?

Wrap the Izaar around the waist, secure with a belt, and drape the Ridaa over both shoulders. During Tawaf, slide the Ridaa under the right armpit (Idtiba). After Tawaf, return it to covering both shoulders.

Can women wear stitched clothing for Hajj?

Yes. Female pilgrims wear their normal modest clothing during Ihram. The two-piece unstitched Ihram is required only for men. The state of Ihram and its rules of behaviour apply equally to both.

How tight should the Izaar be?

Snug enough not to slip, loose enough to walk comfortably. A simple belt or money belt over the Izaar prevents slipping during Tawaf and Sa'i.

Can I sleep in Ihram?

Yes. Pilgrims sleep in Ihram throughout the rites at Mina, Arafah, and Muzdalifah. A spare set is useful so one can air out while you wear the other.

Do I have to wash my Ihram before I fly?

Yes, practically speaking, you should. New Ihram cloth from any brand ships with finishing chemicals from manufacturing. One home wash with mild detergent removes residue and softens the fabric so it doesn't chafe during Tawaf.

Conclusion

Wearing Ihram correctly is one small piece of a much larger journey, but it's one of the first practical decisions you make at Miqat. Practising at home, pre-washing the cloth, and bringing a spare are the three preparations that turn a first-time experience into a smooth one.
