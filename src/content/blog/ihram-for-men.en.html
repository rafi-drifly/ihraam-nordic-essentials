If you are preparing for Umrah or Hajj for the first time, the two plain white sheets can feel like the most unfamiliar part of the whole journey. This guide covers what ihram is, how to wear the izar and rida, which fabric and size to choose, what footwear is allowed, and how to keep everything secure while you walk, pray, and perform tawaf.

Everything here is written plainly. Where scholars differ, we point you to your group leader or a trusted scholar rather than guessing on your behalf.

What exactly is the ihram, and what are the two sheets?

For men, ihram refers to two things at once. First, it is the sacred state a pilgrim enters before Umrah or Hajj, marked by the intention (niyyah) and by following the rules of that state. Second, in everyday speech "ihram" also means the clothing itself: two unstitched white cloths worn instead of normal, tailored clothes.

  • The izar, the lower cloth, wrapped around the waist and covering from above the navel down past the knees.
  • The rida, the upper cloth, draped over the shoulders and upper body.

The garments are deliberately simple and identical for everyone. A king and a labourer stand before God in the same plain white cloth, which is one of the quiet lessons of ihram.

How do I wear the izar and rida step by step?

Before dressing, most pilgrims perform ghusl (a full ritual wash) and may trim the nails and remove unwanted hair beforehand, because these become restricted once in ihram.

Wearing the izar (lower sheet)

  • Stand with your feet slightly apart and hold the sheet behind your lower back, gripping the top corners in each hand.
  • Bring the cloth around your waist so it sits above your navel, wrapping one side across, then the other.
  • Fold or roll the top edge over along your waistline a few times to lock it in place.
  • If you use a belt, fasten it a couple of inches below the top edge so the cloth cannot slip.

Wearing the rida (upper sheet)

Drape the second cloth over both shoulders so it covers your back and chest. A common style is idtiba during tawaf, where the middle of the rida is placed under the right armpit and both ends thrown over the left shoulder, leaving the right shoulder bare. Outside of tawaf, both shoulders are normally covered.

Finally, face the Kaaba's direction and make your intention for Umrah or Hajj in your heart. That intention is what formally begins the state of ihram. For a fuller walkthrough, see our guide on how to wear ihram step by step.

What fabric is best, terry towel, cotton, or waffle weave?

There is no religious requirement for a particular fabric. The cloth simply needs to be unstitched, clean, and modest (not see-through). Comfort is where the choice really matters, because you may be in these sheets for many hours in the heat.

  • Terry towel, plush looped surface, very absorbent, and good opacity so it never turns translucent in bright sunlight. The trade-off is that it is warmer and heavier, and lower-quality versions can shed lint.
  • Waffle (honeycomb) weave, lighter, dries faster, and lets more air through, which suits Makkah's heat. It can be slightly more sheer than terry in very bright light, so opacity is worth checking.
  • Flat / plain weave, thin and easy to pack small, though generally less absorbent.

The practical rule of thumb: terry for opacity and absorbency, waffle for airflow and quick drying, flat for packing light. Many pilgrims in hot seasons lean toward lighter, breathable weaves and simply carry a spare.

What size and dimensions should I get for my height and build?

Ihram sheets are usually sold by size so the cloth wraps fully and drapes without gaping. As a general guide used across the market:

  • A standard adult size suits most men of average height and build.
  • Taller or larger-built men should size up so the izar wraps around comfortably with enough overlap, and the rida covers the back and chest without pulling tight.

The two things to get right are length (enough to wrap the waist with a secure overlap) and drape (the rida should sit over both shoulders comfortably). If you are between sizes, size up: a larger sheet is easier to fold and tuck than a small one is to stretch. Check the sizing information on the exact product you are buying, since dimensions vary between brands.

Can I wear underwear or a belt underneath?

Underwear: The classic position is that a man in ihram should not wear stitched or fitted garments, and standard underwear is fitted and sewn to the body, so most scholars say it should not be worn while in ihram. Some contemporary scholars have discussed exceptions in cases of genuine need, so if you have a specific medical or continence concern, ask your group's scholar directly rather than assuming.

Belt: A plain belt or money pouch worn to hold the izar in place, or to carry documents and cash, is widely permitted. It is not a tailored garment covering the body; it is a fastening. Ihram belts with pockets are popular for exactly this reason and make walking and tawaf far less stressful.

What footwear is allowed?

The general principle is that footwear should leave the top of the foot uncovered. Sandals, flip-flops, and open slides are the safe, standard choice. Where the schools differ is on how much of the foot must be exposed:

  • The Maliki, Shafi'i, and Hanbali schools treat the ka'b as the ankle bone, so footwear that leaves the ankle and instep exposed is fine.
  • The Hanafi school treats it as the raised middle bone of the instep (the mid-foot), so, to be safe, footwear should not cover that bone.

In plain terms: open sandals that expose the top and middle of the foot satisfy every school. Avoid trainers, closed shoes, and anything that covers the instep. Choose a comfortable, well-broken-in pair, because you will walk a great deal on hard marble.

How many sets do I need for Umrah vs Hajj?

For Umrah: One good set is technically enough, but two is the practical minimum. A spare means you are covered if one gets wet, soiled, or damaged.

For Hajj: Plan for two to three sets at least. Hajj spans several days across Mina, Arafat, and Muzdalifah in intense heat, so you will sweat heavily and want to change. We cover this in depth in how many ihrams you need for Hajj and ihram for Umrah vs Hajj.

How do I keep it secure while walking, praying, and doing tawaf?

  • Fold the waistband properly. Roll the top edge over two or three times rather than tucking it once. A rolled band grips itself.
  • Use an ihram belt. A fabric belt fastened just below the fold is the single most effective fix and doubles as secure storage.
  • Check before tawaf and sa'i. Re-tighten before each strenuous section so you are not adjusting mid-crowd.
  • Mind the rida during prostration. Keep a hand ready to steady the upper cloth as you go down and up in sujood.

If it does slip and you briefly become exposed, cover yourself immediately. According to the majority position, an accidental, promptly corrected exposure during tawaf does not invalidate your tawaf or break your ihram. We explain this fully in the ihram rules guide.

Can ihram be washed and reused?

Yes. Ihram sheets are ordinary cloth with no sacred property in the material itself, so they can be washed and reused for future pilgrimages. Wash with unscented detergent, use a gentle cycle to protect the weave, and dry fully. For a complete routine, including whether to wash before you fly, see wash your ihram before you fly.

Frequently asked questions

Do the sheets have to be pure white?

White is the strong sunnah and the near-universal practice for men, and it is what you should buy. The essential requirement is unstitched, clean, modest cloth.

Can I wear underwear under ihram?

Standard underwear is stitched and fitted to the body, so most scholars say men should not wear it in ihram. A plain ihram belt to hold the izar is allowed. For a specific medical need, ask a scholar.
