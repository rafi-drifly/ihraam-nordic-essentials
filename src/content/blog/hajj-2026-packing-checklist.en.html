What to pack for Hajj 2026: the essentials

Everything in this section is required or near-required. Skipping any one of these will cost you time or money on the ground.

Ihram and clothing for the rites

  • Two Ihram sets minimum (one to wear, one spare). Three if travelling with family. Pure Ihram sells the 2-pack at EUR 37 and ships from Sweden with free EU delivery.
  • Belt for the Izaar (the waist piece). A simple money belt or Ihram belt works.
  • Sandals or flip-flops with no covered toes, required for Ihram rules.
  • Modest, loose-fitting clothing for non-Ihram days in Makkah and Madinah.
  • A light shawl or blanket for cold mornings at Muzdalifah.

Documents and money

  • Passport with at least 6 months validity remaining.
  • Hajj visa (issued via your authorised Hajj agency).
  • Vaccination certificate, including required meningococcal ACWY (check current Saudi entry rules close to your travel date).
  • Printed copy of your hotel and flight bookings.
  • Local currency (Saudi Riyal) for incidentals. Most major purchases accept card, but cash matters at Mina.

Health and hygiene

  • Personal medications (with prescriptions in original packaging).
  • Unscented soap, shampoo, and toothpaste. Scented products are not permitted in the state of Ihram.
  • Sunscreen, unscented if used during Ihram.
  • Lip balm, foot cream, and a small first-aid kit.
  • Reusable water bottle. Hydration during Hajj rites is essential.

What most first-time pilgrims forget

These are the items that consistently get added to suitcases between the first packing pass and the final one. Save yourself the second trip to the shop.

  • A small drawstring bag to carry sandals during Tawaf. You'll want them off but kept close.
  • Pocket-sized dua cards for the rites. Pure Ihram offers free downloadable dua cards designed for Hajj, printable, easy to read, easy to carry.
  • A power bank and the right plug adapter for Saudi outlets (Type G).
  • Wet wipes, useful for everything from cleaning hands to the floors at Mina tents.
  • A simple cross-body bag with a zip, for documents, phone, and dua cards. Pickpockets exist; zips help.
  • Spare prescription glasses if you wear them.
  • A printed copy of your travel itinerary in case your phone dies.

What to leave at home

Less is more. Hajj involves walking long distances, packed crowds, and limited room in your accommodation. Anything not on the essential list should stay home unless it solves a problem you'll definitely encounter.

Specifically leave: heavy electronics beyond your phone and a backup, scented toiletries, formal clothing (none required), large suitcases that won't fit in cramped Mina tents, jewellery, and any items prohibited under Saudi customs (alcohol, pork products, certain religious texts not pre-approved).

How to pack for the journey from Europe

European pilgrims have one advantage: a relatively short flight. Use this practical packing structure for the trip from Sweden, the Nordics, or Western Europe.

  • Carry-on: documents, money, medications, one Ihram set (in case checked luggage is delayed), phone and charger, water bottle.
  • Checked bag: spare Ihram set, clothing, toiletries, sandals, prayer items.
  • Wear: comfortable travel clothes that you can change out of into Ihram at the appropriate Miqat. Many pilgrims fly in regular clothes and change before crossing the Miqat.
  • Total weight target: under 20 kg checked, under 7 kg carry-on. Most European-to-Jeddah Hajj flights enforce these limits.

Frequently asked questions

How early should I start packing for Hajj?

Two weeks before departure is the sensible window. That gives time to order anything missing, including spare Ihram sets, which can take a few days to deliver. Pure Ihram ships from Sweden with free EU delivery.

Can I bring my own food on the flight?

Sealed snacks and unopened bottled water through security are usually fine, but check airline rules. Once in Saudi Arabia, food is widely available at Mina, Arafah, and around the Haram.

What size suitcase should I take for Hajj?

Most European pilgrims travel with a 65 to 70 litre checked suitcase plus a small carry-on. Storage at Mina tents is tight, so packing cubes help.

Do I need to pack toilet paper?

Most accommodations and public bathrooms in Saudi Arabia have water (lota) rather than tissue. Bringing a small pack of tissues is sensible.

What's the most overlooked item on a Hajj packing list?

A spare Ihram. Pilgrims consistently underestimate how much wear the cloth takes. A EUR 18 spare set is the simplest insurance available for the trip.

Hajj is the journey of a lifetime, and the packing decisions you make in the days before you fly will shape how present you can be once you arrive. Travel light. Pack the essentials. Bring a spare Ihram.
